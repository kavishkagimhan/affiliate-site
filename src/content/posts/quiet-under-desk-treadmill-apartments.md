## Understanding Treadmill Noise

### Sources of Noise

**1. Motor Noise**
- Primary noise source
- Varies by motor type and quality
- Brushless motors quieter than brushed
- Higher RPM = more noise

**2. Belt Friction**
- Belt sliding over deck
- Poor lubrication increases noise
- Belt quality matters
- Alignment issues create sound

**3. Structural Vibration**
- Frame resonance
- Poor build quality amplifies
- Floor contact transmits vibration
- Loose parts rattle

**4. Footfall Impact**
- Your steps hitting the belt
- Unavoidable but reducible
- Heavier users create more sound
- Running vs. walking difference

### Decibel Levels Explained

Understanding noise measurements:

**Decibel (dB) Scale:**
- **20-30 dB:** Whisper, library
- **30-40 dB:** Quiet conversation
- **40-50 dB:** Moderate office noise
- **50-60 dB:** Normal conversation
- **60-70 dB:** Vacuum cleaner
- **70+ dB:** Loud, potentially disruptive

**For Apartments:**
- **<45 dB:** Excellent, whisper-quiet
- **45-50 dB:** Very good, quiet conversation level
- **50-55 dB:** Acceptable, moderate noise
- **>55 dB:** Too loud for most apartments

**Our Recommendation:** Target under 50dB for apartment use, under 45dB for shared spaces or video calls.

## Noise Reduction Strategies for Apartments

Even the quietest treadmill benefits from these strategies:

### 1. Use a Quality Mat

**Benefits:**
- Absorbs vibration
- Prevents floor damage
- Reduces noise transmission
- Provides stability on carpet

**Recommended Mats:**
- **Rubber exercise mats:** Best vibration dampening
- **Puzzle mats:** Good, affordable option
- **Premium treadmill mats:** Maximum protection

**Thickness:**
- Minimum 6mm for basic protection
- 8-10mm for better noise reduction
- 12mm+ for maximum dampening

**Cost:** $30-$80 CAD

### 2. Floor Placement

**Best Locations:**
- Over concrete (basements)
- On main floor (not above bedrooms)
- Away from shared walls
- In corners (less sound transmission)

**Avoid:**
- Directly above bedrooms
- Against shared walls
- Near apartments with families
- Over hollow floors

### 3. Time Your Workouts

**Best Times:**
- Mid-morning (9 AM - 12 PM)
- Afternoon (2 PM - 6 PM)
- Early evening (6 PM - 8 PM)

**Avoid:**
- Early morning (before 8 AM)
- Late night (after 9 PM)
- Weekend early mornings
- During quiet hours (check lease)

### 4. Regular Maintenance

**Reduce Noise Through:**
- **Lubrication:** Keep belt well-lubricated
- **Tightening:** Secure all bolts/screws
- **Cleaning:** Remove debris from belt
- **Alignment:** Keep belt centered
- **Inspection:** Check for worn parts

**Schedule:**
- Quick check: Weekly

- Professional service: Annually

### 5. Proper Usage Technique
